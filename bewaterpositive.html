Project Overview
I designed the "Be Water Positive" campaign, a partnership between the International Indian Film Academy (IIFA) and AquaKraft, for the annual 2023 award show held in Abu Dhabi, UAE. This project aimed to inform people about the water crisis in India and raise awareness about water conservation. By creating engaging visuals and compelling stories, the campaign successfully reached millions, emphasising the importance of sustainable water practices and making a significant impact on public awareness.
My Role
My role in the "Be Water Positive" campaign was to create the main logo and design the website. I developed a striking logo that conveyed the campaign's message of sustainability. The website provided an engaging user experience with compelling visuals and clear information, serving as the central hub for the campaign's details.

What Did I Learn?
1. Collaboration: I worked from Vancouver while my team was in Mumbai and Abu Dhabi. Managing different time zones taught me how to prioritize tasks and streamline our work processes for smooth teamwork.
2. Trusting my design instincts: As the only designer on the team, I learned to trust my creative decisions. This project helped me confidently explain why I chose certain designs, gaining support from my team for our campaign goals.
3. Designing perfectly for the global audience: With 71 million viewers engaging with the "Be Water Positive" campaign, maintaining brand consistency was crucial. Despite working remotely from Vancouver while the team operated from Mumbai and Abu Dhabi, I learned to manage details meticulously and uphold the campaign's standards. This experience strengthened my ability to deliver high-quality work under pressure, ensuring our message reached and resonated with a vast global audience.
